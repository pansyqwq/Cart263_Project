# Today I met a Shooting Star: Response on Group 1

## by Ben Woschitz

I got assigned to evaluate Group 1’s project: today I met a shooting star. Initial impressions over the presentation were positive as I will get into later. However, I managed to acquire the link for the game and tried to load it into my own browser, which didn’t work. After clicking “Start,” the game would not load. It would perpetually be stuck in a loading screen, as checking the console reveals that it cannot read the properties of background. This isn’t great, as I would not be able to play through the game myself in order to evaluate, so I will have to go off of memory. There is probably some issue with the Github upload as it seemed to work perfectly fine during the presentation, so something is different between the two, or maybe it’s the browser, I’m not sure.
Regardless, it was quite an interesting concept and narrative story, though I am confused regarding certain aspects. The biggest draw of the entire game were the images, they were very intriguing and fit well despite the very basic UI around the dialogue. It felt almost eerie? Not in the traditional sense that we think of, but in a more mystical way. There was a sense of emptiness and longing conveyed by the overall visual style of the project where the images did a lot of the heavy lifting to convey these sentiments, along with the desaturated and neutral tones present within. The images were created from film which I found super neat and added an element of realism, as if they were taken by a person going through the journey (you) with one of those older polaroid cameras. With that being said, one of the most confusing elements was the story itself. It was clear through the presentation that it was a story either about finding something, or finding one’s self. At least that is the impression I got from it. Although, it wasn’t super clear as to what the exact purpose was. It’s unfortunate that I’m unable to play it through the link as I won’t be able to decipher this myself. Furthermore, I was even more confused by the presence of the bottle caps containing a random message. Their placement seemed odd to me. From a technical level, it was nice adding another layer of interactivity for the user/player. I’m also glad they were able to keep track of whether the player collected them or not as they played through, despite the difficulties they mentioned in class. The caps themselves sort of took me out of the story during the presentation and seemed out of place in a visual sense. They didn’t seem like they belonged and I still am not sure as to what the point of them being included aside from breaking up the monotony of continuously clicking through dialogue. Overall, the story seemed to have a coherent although vague theme that matched with the visuals and delivered for an interesting project. There seem to be technical issues that prevent me from going through it myself, unfortunately.
